### Abstract
Version 2.6 of the Atimo Theorem describes a heuristic emergent field model in which spacetime geometry is interpreted as an effective projection of a dimensionless informational scalar field $\mathcal{I}$. The field is described by a scalar Lagrangian density with a nonlinear stability potential. The dynamics possess two stable minima, interpreted as pre-metric and metric stabilized states, as well as an unstable transition barrier. Local matter is modeled via a phenomenological coupling as a source term. For galactic scales, a logarithmic far-field potential is proposed to address flat rotation curves. The quantitative derivation of lensing effects and a fully covariant matter coupling remain subjects for further research.

### 1. Definition of the Information Field ($\mathcal{I}$)
The central variable $\mathcal{I}$ is defined as a dimensionless scalar field of coupling density.
* **Pre-metric State**: Prior to metric emergence, the field is heuristically described as a maximally correlated pre-metric informational state. A quantum mechanical interpretation as EPR-like entanglement requires a later explicit Hilbert space formulation.
* **Interpretative Terms**: The terms *resonance*, *reflection*, and *latency* serve as descriptive metaphors for the pre-metric dynamics. The operative definition is based exclusively on the scalar field $\mathcal{I}$, its potential, and its coupling to matter density.

### 2. Field Dynamics & Lagrangian Density
Under a suitable sign convention, the Lagrangian density $\mathcal{L}$ is extended by a linear coupling term:

$$\mathcal{L} = \frac{1}{2} \partial_\mu \mathcal{I} \partial^\mu \mathcal{I} - V(\mathcal{I}) + \kappa \rho \mathcal{I}$$

This results in the effective field equation:

$$\square \mathcal{I} + 2\lambda\mathcal{I}(\mathcal{I}-1)(2\mathcal{I}-1) = \kappa \rho$$

**Dimensional Analysis**: Since $\mathcal{I}$ is dimensionless, $\lambda$ and $\kappa \rho$ have the dimension of field curvature ($L^{-2}$) in geometric units. The parameter $r_c$ has the dimension of length ($L$). If $\Phi_{gal}$ is used as a classical potential, $\alpha$ has the dimension of a squared velocity ($L^2 T^{-2}$).

### 3. The Stability Potential $V(\mathcal{I})$
For $\lambda > 0$, the potential $V(\mathcal{I}) = \lambda(\mathcal{I}^2 - \mathcal{I})^2$ defines the energetic topology of the field. Three stationary points emerge:
* $\mathcal{I} = 0$: Stable pre-metric minimum (geometrically inactive ground state).
* $\mathcal{I} = 1/2$: Unstable transition barrier (energetic maximum).
* $\mathcal{I} = 1$: Stabilized metric fixed point (minimum of physical reality).

### 4. Metric Structure
Spacetime geometry is initially described via a dimensionless conformal scale factor $\varphi(\mathcal{I})$:

$$g_{\mu\nu} = (1 + \varphi(\mathcal{I})) \eta_{\mu\nu}$$

**Constraint**: Since null geodesics can be structurally invariant under conformal transformations, sufficient light deflection is not automatically guaranteed by this factor alone. Quantitative description of lensing effects requires an extension of the metric or explicit photon coupling.

### 5. Galactic Application (Phenomenological Approach)
For spherically symmetric systems, a logarithmic effective galactic potential $\Phi_{gal}$ is proposed. For $\alpha > 0$, an attractive radial additional acceleration results:

$$\Phi_{gal}(r) = \alpha \ln\left(1 + \frac{r}{r_c}\right)$$

This leads to the radial acceleration $a(r)$ and the orbital velocity $v$:

1. **Acceleration**: $a(r) = -d\Phi_{gal}/dr = -\frac{\alpha}{r + r_c}$
2. **Velocity**: For $r \gg r_c$, it follows that $v^2 = r \cdot |a(r)| \approx \alpha$ (asymptotically constant).

The derivation of the function $\Phi_{gal}(r)$ from a concrete solution $\mathcal{I}(r)$ of the field equation and a specified mapping $\varphi(\mathcal{I})$ remains a future development step.

### 1. Definition des Informationsfeldes ($\mathcal{I}$)
Die zentrale Variable $\mathcal{I}$ wird als dimensionsloses Skalarfeld der Kopplungsdichte definiert.
* **Prämetrischer Zustand**: Vor der metrischen Emergenz wird das Feld heuristisch als maximal korrelierter prämetrischer Informationszustand beschrieben. Eine quantenmechanische Interpretation als EPR-artige Verschränkung erfordert eine spätere explizite Hilbertraumformulierung.
* **Interpretative Begriffe**: Die Begriffe Resonanz, Spiegelung und Latenz dienen als beschreibende Metaphern der prämetrischen Dynamik. Die operative Definition erfolgt ausschließlich über das Skalarfeld $\mathcal{I}$, dessen Potential und die Kopplung an die Materiedichte.

### 2. Feld-Dynamik & Lagrange-Dichte
Unter geeigneter Vorzeichenkonvention wird die Lagrange-Dichte $\mathcal{L}$ um einen linearen Kopplungsterm erweitert:

$$\mathcal{L} = \frac{1}{2} \partial_\mu \mathcal{I} \partial^\mu \mathcal{I} - V(\mathcal{I}) + \kappa \rho \mathcal{I}$$

Daraus ergibt sich die effektive Feldgleichung:

$$\square \mathcal{I} + 2\lambda\mathcal{I}(\mathcal{I}-1)(2\mathcal{I}-1) = \kappa \rho$$

**Dimensionsanalyse**: Da $\mathcal{I}$ dimensionslos ist, besitzen $\lambda$ und $\kappa\rho$ in geometrischen Einheiten die Dimension der Feldkrümmung ($L^{-2}$). Der Parameter $r_c$ besitzt die Dimension einer Länge ($L$). Wird $\Phi_{gal}$ als klassisches Potential verwendet, besitzt $\alpha$ die Dimension einer quadrierten Geschwindigkeit ($L^2 T^{-2}$).

### 3. Das Stabilitätspotential $V(\mathcal{I})$
Für $\lambda > 0$ definiert das Potential $V(\mathcal{I}) = \lambda(\mathcal{I}^2 - \mathcal{I})^2$ die energetische Topologie des Feldes. Es ergeben sich drei stationäre Punkte:
* $\mathcal{I} = 0$: Stabiles prämetrisches Minimum (geometrisch inaktiver Grundzustand).
* $\mathcal{I} = 1/2$: Instabile Übergangsbarriere (energetisches Maximum).
* $\mathcal{I} = 1$: Stabilisierter metrischer Fixpunkt (Minimum der physikalischen Realität).

$$g_{\mu\nu} = (1 + \varphi(\mathcal{I})) \eta_{\mu\nu}$$
